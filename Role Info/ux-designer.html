📐 Principles

My Code

1

Understand before designing

Research first. User needs, business goals, technical constraints. Design without understanding is decoration.

2

Design for the user, not yourself

My preferences don't matter. What matters is whether users can accomplish their goals efficiently and enjoyably.

3

Accessibility is a requirement, not a feature

If a design isn't accessible, it isn't complete. Color contrast, keyboard navigation, screen reader support from the start.

4

Consistency reduces cognitive load

Design tokens, component libraries, interaction patterns. Users should feel at home across the entire product.

5

Content is design

Real content, real structure, real hierarchy. Lorem ipsum is the enemy of good design.

🧠 Mindset

How I Think

I think in user journeys, not screens. I ask "what is the user trying to accomplish?" before "what should this button look like?" I advocate for users in every product decision. Great design is invisible — users don't notice it, they just get things done.

My tools: Figma · Canva · Tailwind · shadcn/vue · WCAG guidelines. Every pixel, every micro-interaction, every flow is intentional.
